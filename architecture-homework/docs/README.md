# PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Music teachers often spend a significant amount of time at the end of the academic year and on each vacation completing administrative work—documenting lesson plans, listing participation in concerts, competitions, and webinars to create a report. This process is repetitive, time-consuming, and often disorganized, especially given the large number of events and extracurricular activities involved in music education.

The goal of this project is to simplify and automate these tasks, allowing music teachers to focus on their creative and educational work during the year—and to actually rest at the beginning of summer instead of dealing with paperwork.

We propose a platform for tracking teacher activities and event participation, specifically designed for music educators. The system will automatically collect and organize data such as:

- lessons conducted
- students performances and concerts
- professional development (webinars, trainings)
- certificates and achievements

Based on this data, the platform will generate structured, ready-to-submit reports, reducing manual effort and minimizing errors. By centralizing all information in one place, the system will streamline end-of-year reporting and significantly reduce administrative burden.

# ASSUMPTIONS

1. **Reduced human error**  
   Automating data collection and report generation will minimize manual input, reducing mistakes, inconsistencies, and missing information.
2. **Improved teacher well-being**  
   By reducing administrative workload, teachers will have more time for personal life, rest, and family—especially during high-stress periods like the end of the academic year.
3. **Happier and more productive students**  
   Teachers happier - students happier.
4. **Faster report generation**  
   Reports that currently take hours or days to compile due to teachers remembering everything will be generated instantly or within minutes using pre-collected data.
5. **Centralized data management**  
   All teacher-related activities (lessons, events, certificates, achievements) will be stored in one place, eliminating the need to search across emails, files, and messaging apps.
6. **Increased productivity during the school year**  
   Teachers will spend less time on documentation and more time on teaching, student engagement, and creative work.
7. **Higher data accuracy and consistency**  
   Standardized report formats will ensure that all required information is included and presented uniformly.
8. **Better visibility of teacher achievements**  
   The platform will make it easier to track and showcase participation in concerts, competitions, and professional development activities.
9. **Adoption depends on ease of use**  
   If the platform is not significantly simpler than current workflows, teachers may resist using it—especially those less comfortable with technology.
